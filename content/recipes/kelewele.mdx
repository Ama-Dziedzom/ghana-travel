---
title: "Kelewele (Spiced Fried Plantains)"
slug: "kelewele-spiced-plantains"
category: "street-food"
description: "The ultimate Ghanaian street snack—sweet ripe plantains marinated in ginger and spices, then fried to perfection."
coverImage: "/images/recipe_kelewele.png"
prepTime: 15
cookTime: 10
servings: 4
difficulty: "easy"
ingredients:
  - "4 very ripe plantains (yellow with black spots)"
  - "2 tbsp fresh ginger, grated"
  - "1 small onion, finely minced"
  - "1 tsp chili powder or cayenne"
  - "1/2 tsp ground cloves"
  - "1/2 tsp salt"
  - "Vegetable oil for deep frying"
instructions:
  - "Peel the plantains and cut them into small cubes or diagonal slices."
  - "In a bowl, mix the ginger, onion, chili powder, cloves, and salt into a paste."
  - "Toss the plantain cubes in the spice paste until evenly coated. Let it marinate for at least 15 minutes."
  - "Heat oil in a deep pan. Fry the plantains in batches until they are golden brown and slightly caramelized at the edges."
  - "Drain on paper towels and serve hot."
tips: "Serve with roasted peanuts for the authentic roadside experience. If you can't find very ripe plantains, wait until they are almost black—that's when they are sweetest!"
---

You'll find Kelewele vendors on almost every corner in Accra as soon as the sun goes down. The smell of frying ginger is the signal that the night has begun.
